RISE WITH ENTREPRENUERSHIP

About

The goal of this study is to analyze the impact of social networks on the desire of individuals to pursue entrepreneurship and gain important resources. Our research will explore how socially disadvantaged entrepreneurs will build their start-ups once they are exposed to more social networks. The current project focuses on the changes in an entrepreneur’s risk attitude once they are exposed to an increase in social networking opportunities.

Our research team is composed of professors and research scholars with deep expertise in the economics of innovation and experimentation. Our research projects have been funded by the NSF, NASA, the Kauffman Foundation, and The Innovation Growth Lab. PIs hold affiliations with Rice University, USC, MIT, and the National Bureau of Economic Research. See our biography section for more information on the research team.

Benefits

The Key Features of our Initiative

LOGISTICS

Participants in this study will be provided travel reimbursements to networking events, and will also be assigned a networking buddy that will accompany them to these events. This will include receiving free Uber and Lyft rides to various networking events.

EXPERT ADVICE

Networking allows people to receive advice about their business ideas from like-minded individuals who are already well established in their field. Through networking, participants in this study will be able to better tailor their business ideas for success.

BUILDING CONNECTIONS

Through meeting new people also interested in entrepreneurship, people will be able to build connections with others that can help them in pursuing their start-up. By networking with people in the same industry, and also different industries, participants will be able to expand their network and increase their knowledge of the field.

INCREASE VISIBILITY

Through networking and discussing business ideas with other people, participants in this study will increase their visibility and build their reputation. Every new person met at a networking event is a potential new customer that may be interested in a participant’s business idea.
