About

The goal of this research is to study how the inclusion of more successful entrepreneurs in the social networks of prospective entrepreneurs changes their risk preferences and business planning. We are currently looking for potential candidates for our research experiment. In the experiment, we will provide a group of fledgling entrepreneurs with free online or in-person networking opportunities and a networking “buddy”. We also offer Uber or Lyft credits to facilitate transportation to the networking events. Subjects in the experiment will complete demographic and company background surveys and their business behavior will be studied.

Our research team is composed of professors and research scholars from Rice University, University of Southern California (USC), and Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), with deep expertise in the science of innovation and experimentation (see the biography section for more information). The research project is funded by NSF, NASA, Kauffman foundation, and the Innovation Growth Lab.

If you are interested in entrepreneurship, we encourage you to sign up! The recruiting process will continue until the end of summer 2020 and the experiment will start in fall 2020. Feel free to reach out if you have any questions.

Benefits

The Key Features of our Initiative

LOGISTICS

Participants in this study will be provided travel reimbursements to networking events, and will also be assigned a networking buddy that will accompany them to these events. This will include receiving free Uber and Lyft rides to events.

EXPERT ADVICE

Networking allows people to receive advice about their business ideas from like-minded individuals who are already well established in their field. Through networking, participants in this study will be able to better tailor their business ideas for success.

BUILDING CONNECTIONS

Through meeting new people also interested in entrepreneurship, participants will be able to build connections with others, learn from the experiences of others, and increase their knowledge of the field.

INCREASE VISIBILITY

Through networking and discussing business ideas with others, participants will increase their visibility and build their reputation. Every new person met at a networking event is a potential new customer that may be interested in a participant’s business idea.

Team

Yael Hochberg - Ralph S. O'Connor Professor of Entrepreneurship and Finance, Rice University (@yaelhochberg)

Professor Hochberg's research and teaching interests are focused on entrepreneurship, innovation, and the financing of entrepreneurial activity. Her research focuses on the venture capital industry, accelerators, networks and corporate governance and compensation policies. In addition to her doctorate in finance from Stanford, she holds a B.Sc. in Industrial Engineering and Management from the Technion-Israel Institute of Technology and an A.M. in Economics from Stanford University. Her research has been published in top tier journals, including Science Magazine, the Journal of Finance, the Review of Financial Studies, the Journal of Accounting Research, and the Journal of Financial Economics, and has been presented at numerous universities and governmental bodies around the world. She is an Associate Editor at the Journal of Banking and Finance and the Journal of Empirical Finance, and previously served as an Associate Editor at the Review of Finance.
